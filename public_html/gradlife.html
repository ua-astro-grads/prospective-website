Student Life

It would be impossible to write down all of the activities our graduate students participate in outside of their research and classes, but here you can find a good number of them organized into broad categories. If you have an interest or a hobby not mentioned here, ask the current graduate students to see if anyone else knows about it too! We're a big group of grad students, there's a good chance one of us knows.

We have also included on the Gallery page an assortment of photos showing us having fun within Tucson, across Arizona, and around the World!

Tucson

Tucson is the second most populous city in Arizona with just over 1 million inhabitants in the entire metropolitan area. Tucson is located in the Sonoran Desert, which makes it a very predictable climate to live in. Every season (except summer) boasts exceptional weather (see graphic below). Yes, it's hot in the summer months, but residents of Tucson often escape the summer heat by heading up to nearby Mount Lemmon, which, at 9157 ft (2791 m), boasts summer highs in the 70s °F (low 20s °C) and snow in the winter months.

Living Expenses

Compared to other cities in the US, Tucson still has a relatively low cost of living. Graduate students typically spend between $600 and $1100 a month on rent, including utilities, and spend anywhere between $200 and $500 on groceries a month. Many graduate students live with a roommate, or two, but quite a few choose to live alone. Students typically live within five miles of campus, which can be reached easily by car, bike, public transportation, or even foot. If you choose to stay with a grad student host during your visit, you'll get a chance to see how and where grad students at Steward live, but make sure to ask other current students to show off where they live regardless!

The Outdoors

Thanks to the amazing weather, many graduate students spend their free time outdoors. Groups of students regularly go hiking, rock climbing, camping, and bicycling (road and mountain!) in the nearby Catalina Mountains or take longer trips to the many beautiful parks around Arizona. When it gets too hot in the summer, we either turn nocturnal, spend more time indoors, or find higher elevation to cool off. In the winters, groups of students take skiing trips to the nearby ski-town on Mt.Lemmon or drive up to Flagstaff! Tucson is also an incredibly friendly bike city, with many roads having large dedicated bike lanes. Many graduate students don't own cars and get around on bikes, and cycle recreationally as well! Tucson is surrounded by over 100 miles of paved pedestrian and bike-only paths, known as “The Loop”. Pre-pandemic, graduate students, postdocs, and faculty spend a full day in the Spring biking all over Tucson on a tour of local breweries, known as El Tour de Biere and plans are in place to bring this back.

Indoor Activities

The outdoors isn’t for everyone, and that’s okay! Tucson has lots of options for indoor fun as well. Many students pass their free time playing video games and/or board games, either on their own or with other students. Multiple D&D groups, ranging from beginner to advanced, have been established among the grad students, but we're always looking for new campaigners! Groups of graduate students also attend weekly pub Trivia Nights. If you love to read, the Tucson Public Library system is really good, especially for a smaller city, and there are several branches all across Tucson. Many of our current grads are avid readers and several book clubs exist throughout the department.

Athletics

Steward graduate students are involved in a variety of sports within the University and outside of it! We usually scrape together passable intramural volleyball and softball teams (and one of our softball teams won the entire league last year!), and some students are involved in local ultimate frisbee and soccer leagues. At one point there was a large contingent of students practicing aerial silks at the local circus academy, and we even have a student or two who fence with the University team!

Arts

We also like to spend our time getting up to various creative activities, like pottery, crochet, sketching, knitting, embroidery, painting, photography, and even glass blowing! Craft nights and paint nights are popular get-togethers. If acting, movie making, or comedy is a passion of yours, you might be interested in a beloved Steward tradition known as the Grad Play. At the end of each academic year, graduate students come together to write, record, act in, and produce a series of skits poking light fun at department happenings over the last year! In addition, some Broadway musicals and plays occasionally come to Tucson (e.g. Les Misérables, The Book of Mormon, and Silent Sky), and graduate students get discounts on tickets. Tucson has a vibrant artisan community; in the cooler months, there are frequent outdoor artisan markets where grad students can shop unique finds from local artists at reasonable prices. Finally, Tucson is a beautiful city to look at. With over 80 murals around town, finding them all is a great way to get to know the city.

Food

The food in Tucson is some of the best in the world! In 2016, UNESCO designated Tucson as a World City of Gastronomy. The local Mexican food is unparalleled and there are good spots for many kinds of cuisine across the city. If you love food, Tucson is the place to be! Many of our graduate students also enjoy cooking and will frequently bring treats into the department to share. The past few years, the grad students have planned a Thanksgiving potluck for the whole department. It was so delicious, it has become an annual tradition!

Night Life

Tucson has a wonderful downtown with many bars and restaurants! On Fridays, graduate students choose a bar downtown to relax and hang out after work. One of the most popular spots to enjoy the nightlife is Hotel Congress, which has delicious nachos, great drinks, and an outdoor dancing area. We obviously can't list everything here, but do ask us on slack or when you visit about night life.

International Students

Steward is home to a large number of international students, postdoc, and faculty from everywhere aound the world (Belgium, China, Greece, India, Iran, Italy, Malaysia, South Korea, Taiwan, etc.). We understand that international students might have a more difficult time after moving to an unfamiliar place. And there is the SODEI International Scholars Task Force that helps to promote international scholars' well-being and to bridge people from various cultures (both international and domestic). An International Scholars Allyship Program has been launched is help incoming international scholars with their tranistion to US and integration into our community. Thinking about something to do during a weekend or holiday? Check their events/social activities!
